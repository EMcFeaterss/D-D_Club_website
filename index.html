Dungeons and Dragons (D&D for short) is a roleplaying expeience in which a party consisting of any number of players goes through a story that has been made for this reason. The one who makes this story (also called a campaign), in addition to running it, is known as the Dungeon Master (DM for short). The rules of the campaign as well as what you are actually allowed to do is determined by the DM. Whatever the DM says goes. This can lead to fun situations where you can do very stupid things. But the DM can also decide to punsh you for being stupid by making an unfavorable event happen to you in particular.

How do you get through the story? This is solved by a D20 (twenty sided die). Most actions taken by players are determined to be sucessful or not by the D20. For example, if a player rolls a 20 on the die for their action, this is an automatic sucess, often going exactly the way the action was envisioned or better. If a player rolls a 1, that player automatically fails the action, with it usually backfiring on them. There are also bonuses that can be taken into account depending on the player's stats and what they are rolling for.
